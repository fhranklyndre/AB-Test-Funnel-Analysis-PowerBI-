# A/B Test Funnel Analysis & Conversion Optimisation (Power BI)

## Overview

This project analyses the performance of two marketing campaigns (Control vs Test) for Media Reach across a full conversion funnel using Power BI.

The objective was to move beyond surface-level metrics and identify where users drop off, how efficiently they convert, and whether increased spend translates into meaningful business outcomes.

---

## Business Context

Media Reach is running two campaigns to drive user acquisition and conversions. While both campaigns generate traffic and revenue, there is limited visibility into:

- How users behave across the funnel  
- Where conversion inefficiencies exist  
- Whether increased traffic leads to higher-quality outcomes  

Understanding these factors is critical to improving return on marketing spend and overall conversion performance.

---

## Problem Statement

The business lacks a clear understanding of:

- Which campaign delivers better conversion efficiency  
- Where users drop off within the funnel  
- Whether higher spend is improving performance or just increasing volume  
- If the campaigns are attracting high-intent users  

This limits the ability to optimise marketing strategy and user experience effectively.

---

## Project Objectives

- Analyse full-funnel performance from impressions to purchase  
- Identify key drop-off points and bottlenecks  
- Compare Control vs Test campaign performance  
- Evaluate conversion efficiency and cost effectiveness  
- Provide actionable recommendations to improve performance  

---

## Key Metrics Tracked

- Impressions  
- Reach

- Click-Through Rate (CTR)  
- Content Engagement Rate  
- Add-to-Cart Rate  
- Purchase Conversion Rate  
- Cost per Purchase  

---

## Analysis Summary

### Campaign Performance

- Test campaign generated more clicks (181K vs 154K)  
- Purchases increased slightly (16K vs 15K)  
- Overall conversion improved (0.7% vs 0.5%)  

---

### Funnel Behaviour

- Content views remained constant (56K) despite higher traffic  
- Add-to-cart dropped significantly (26K vs 38K)  
- Purchase rate increased sharply in the Test campaign  

---

### Drop-off Analysis

- Largest drop-off occurs immediately after click  
- Test campaign shows higher early-stage drop-off than Control  

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Stronger Acquisition but Weaker Engagement
The Test campaign drives more clicks but does not translate into higher mid-funnel engagement.

### 2. High-Intent User Behaviour
Despite lower engagement:
- Purchases increased  
- Conversion rate improved  

This indicates the Test campaign is attracting more decisive, high-intent users.

### 3. Mid-Funnel Friction
The decline in:
- Content engagement  
- Add-to-cart rate  

suggests inefficiencies in the post-click experience.

### 4. Value Leakage After Click
A significant portion of users drop off immediately after clicking, meaning the business is not fully capitalising on acquired traffic.

---

## Interpretation

The Test campaign improves acquisition and final conversion efficiency but introduces friction earlier in the funnel.

This creates a scenario where:

- Traffic quality improves  
- Conversion improves  
- But user journey efficiency declines  

In essence:

Better traffic, weaker journey, stronger closing.

---

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions
- Optimise landing page experience to align with campaign messaging  
- Improve page load speed and clarity of offer  

### Mid-Funnel Optimisation
- Simplify navigation from content to add-to-cart  
- Strengthen product visibility and value proposition  

### Strategic Direction
- Retain Test campaign targeting approach  
- Combine it with a smoother user journey from the Control campaign  

---

## Future Work

- Implement user segmentation to identify high-value cohorts  
- Track session-level behaviour to understand user interactions post-click  
- Conduct A/B testing on landing page variations  
- Develop predictive models for conversion likelihood  
- Integrate real-time funnel monitoring
